SMJ · Resistance coefficients used by the Pipeline Pressure Drop calculator

ℹ️ K-values are resistance (loss) coefficients in the formula ΔP = K · ½ρv². Default values follow Crane Technical Paper 410 and Idelchik's Handbook of Hydraulic Resistance. Notes on K-values:
K-values here apply to fully open valves and clean fittings at turbulent flow conditions (Re > 10 000). At lower Reynolds numbers, actual losses will be higher. Strainer K-values increase significantly when fouled — always verify with manufacturer pressure-drop curves for final design. For reducers and expanders, K-values are calculated automatically from the Borda-Carnot equation (sudden transitions) based on inlet/outlet diameters entered in the calculator.
